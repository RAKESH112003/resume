## 🎯 Interview Preparation Checklist

### Before Each Interview

- [ ] Research company thoroughly (mission, values, recent news)
- [ ] Review job description and match skills
- [ ] Prepare questions for the interviewer (3-5)
- [ ] Review your resume and relevant projects
- [ ] Practice common interview questions
- [ ] Test tech setup (video, audio, background)
- [ ] Prepare professional appearance
- [ ] Have pen and paper ready for notes

### Common Interview Questions to Prepare

1. **Tell me about yourself**
2. **Why are you interested in this role?**
3. **What are your strengths?**
4. **What are your weaknesses?**
5. **Describe a challenging project you completed**
6. **How do you handle conflict?**
7. **What are your salary expectations?**
8. **Where do you see yourself in 5 years?**
9. **Why should we hire you?**
10. **What questions do you have for us?**

### Role-Specific Questions

**For Data Science/Analytics Roles**:
- Walk through your analytics project from start to finish
- How do you handle missing data?
- What's your approach to A/B testing?
- Explain a complex statistical concept
- How do you communicate technical findings to non-technical stakeholders?

**For Product Manager Roles**:
- How would you improve [company's product]?
- Walk through your product design process
- How do you prioritize features?
- How do you measure success?
- Describe a time you disagreed with engineering

**For Management Roles**:
- How do you build and develop a team?
- Describe your leadership style
- How do you handle underperformance?
- How do you drive innovation in your team?
- Describe a conflict you've managed

## 💬 Follow-up Communication Templates

### Initial Application Follow-up (7-10 days)

```
Subject: Following Up - [Your Name] Application for [Position] at [Company]

Dear [Hiring Manager Name],

I wanted to follow up on my application for the [Position] role at [Company], 
which I submitted on [Date]. I remain very interested in this opportunity and 
would welcome the chance to discuss how my experience in [Key Skills] can 
contribute to your team.

Please let me know if you need any additional information from me.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Post-Interview Thank You

```
Subject: Thank You - [Position] Interview at [Company]

Dear [Interviewer Name],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with me today to discuss the [Position] 
role. I enjoyed learning more about [Company]'s [specific topic discussed] and 
how my background in [relevant skill] aligns with your team's needs.

I'm particularly excited about [specific detail from conversation]. 
I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Rejection Response

```
Subject: Thank You for Considering Me - [Position] at [Company]

Dear [Hiring Manager Name],

Thank you for informing me about the outcome of my application for [Position]. 
While I'm disappointed, I genuinely appreciated the opportunity to learn about 
[Company] and your team's work.

I would welcome the opportunity to stay in touch for future roles that align 
with my background.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## 💡 Tips for Success

1. **Stay Organized**: Update this tracker weekly
2. **Follow Up**: Don't miss deadline dates - set reminders
3. **Personalize**: Customize cover letters for each application
4. **Network**: Reach out to connections at target companies
5. **Interview Prep**: Practice consistently, record yourself
6. **Learn**: After each rejection, reflect on improvements
7. **Celebrate**: Acknowledge progress, even small wins
8. **Balance**: Apply to 3-5 companies/week consistently
9. **Quality Over Quantity**: Focus on good fits, not just volume
10. **Stay Positive**: Job search is a marathon, not a sprint
